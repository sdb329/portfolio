scott joplin orchestral poster

typography III, fall 2021

This poster was inspired by and made for the orchestral piece “Maple Leaf Rag,” originally composed by Scott Joplin. In taking inspiration to the syncopated genre of ragtime, these posters aim to contrast organized, gridded type with a more experimental composition.

the story of an hour

typography III, fall 2021

This was an experimental typography design that was based off of the short story The Story of An Hour by Kate Chopin. The piece is a long, double sided accordion fold with a vertical composition. The reason why this format was chosen was to accurately depict the idea of time passing, and to be able to split up and hide moments of the story that would give away the ending. On the first side, the audience is left on a cliff hanger, and turning the piece around reveals the remainder of the story. The piece is meant to be hung in the round so that both sides can be seen.

souvenir typeface poster

typography II, winter 2021

This poster was a design inspired by the history and development of the souvenir typeface. In combining designs inspired by the time souvenir was made with the influences it picked up along its lifespan, this poster holistically represents the font.

the stag and the ox stall aesop's fables brochure

typography II, winter 2021

This design was a trifold based on a story from “Aesop’s Fables” known as The Stag in the Ox Stall. This brochure effectively uses typography to lead the audience through the story and effectively communicate the mood of the fable.
